# Stewardship Linear Framework

The Stewardship Framework moves from the nature of material custody to the public and intergenerational consequences of how resources are used.

## Phase One: Define Material Responsibility

The reader begins by recognizing that material life is not morally neutral. Ownership, money, provision, debt, savings, and risk form responsibility through limits and consequences.

Chapters: 01-06.

## Phase Two: Order Household Material Life

The reader then considers generosity, wealth, consumption, the body, home, and tools. Stewardship becomes visible in daily custody, maintenance, and use.

Chapters: 07-12.

## Phase Three: Steward Place And Power

The reader applies stewardship to food, land, time, property, waste, technology, infrastructure, enterprise, and capital. Material choices affect neighbors, workers, local communities, and hidden systems.

Chapters: 13-18.

## Phase Four: Hand On What Was Received

The final movement concerns inheritance, poverty, crisis resilience, public goods, ecology, and the stewarded life. The test is whether material patterns remain defensible across years, decades, and generations.

Chapters: 19-24.

## Operating Cadence

Use this cadence when applying the framework:

1. Name what has come into your care.
2. Identify what it costs to maintain.
3. Identify who is affected by its use or neglect.
4. Reverse roles with the person carrying the hidden cost.
5. Name what needs repair, restraint, or provision.
6. Choose one stewardship practice.
7. Review whether the pattern builds resilience.
8. Ask what condition it will be handed on in.
