# Repository Goal

Create a practical framework for money, property, body, home, tools, resources, consumption, inheritance, land, waste, enterprise, infrastructure, and care for the material world downstream of Ethosism. The Stewardship Framework applies objective reality, reciprocity, integrity, long-term responsibility, and contribution to what comes into human custody.

# Writing Direction

When writing or revising this book, follow [docs/writing-guide.md](docs/writing-guide.md). Keep the Stewardship Framework grounded in observable reality, material limits, embodied responsibility, reciprocal moral reasoning, maintenance, repair, provision, generosity, just ownership, and intergenerational care.

The work should be secular and non-theological: it may draw respectfully from religious, philosophical, economic, ecological, civic, household, and practical traditions, but it must not depend on revelation, clergy, supernatural claims, partisan identity, prosperity ideology, anti-wealth resentment, or any single theology for its authority.

Prefer concrete, defensible claims over vague inspiration. Claims about material life should be testable against conduct, consequences, maintenance, reciprocity, incentives, hidden costs, repair, and decade-scale responsibility.

The repo-local Codex skill for this work lives at [.agents/skills/stewardship-writing](.agents/skills/stewardship-writing). Keep Stewardship-specific Codex skills in this repository rather than installing them globally.

# Review Workflow

When reviewing or revising a chapter with the user, proceed paragraph by paragraph. Present one paragraph at a time, wait for the user's edits or approval, apply edits to that paragraph, then move to the next paragraph in the chapter.

Do not batch-review an entire chapter unless the user explicitly asks for a full-pass review. The default review method for this book is interactive paragraph-level editing.
